1.Why is face recognition important?
Face recognition is a highly complex cognitive task that humans rely on and perform on a daily basis. With the rapid technological progress, face recognition is becoming a necessary and pervasive task for the machines to execute. From our smartphones and work environments to airline travel and criminal justice, facial recognition systems (FRS) are embedded in many aspects of our lives

3.What happens when the reference datasets are not diverse?
FRS have been recently scrutinized for their failure to effectively identify certain demographic groups (2, 5, 9). Research suggests that these systems can be especially biased against people of color and women (7). It is shown that in comparison to the identification error rate of 0.8% for white men, FRS fail to identify roughly 35% of the darker-skinned women (3). It has been further argued that the use of FRS may potentially aggravate structural racism and violate basic human rights (8).
