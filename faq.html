1.Why is face recognition important?
Face recognition is a highly complex cognitive task that humans rely on and perform on a daily basis. In today's world, the high demand for face recognition tasks are being primarily met by machine learning systems. As a consequence, from our smartphones and work environments to airline travel, facial recognition systems are becoming a big part of our day to day life. Any biases in these facial recognition systems have the potential to cause unforseen disadvantages for minority groups subject to these biases.
2.What happens when facial recognition systems are biased?
Face recognition systems have been recently criticized for their failure to effectively identify certain demographic groups. Research suggests that these systems can be especially biased against people of color and women. It is shown that in comparison to the identification error rate of 0.8% for lighter-skinned men, face recognition systems fail to identify roughly 35% of the darker-skinned women. It has also been argued that the use of biased face recognition systems may potentially aggravate structural racism and violate basic human rights.

3.What is wrong with current datasets?

Among the existing large facial image databases, each has its own problems. The database may not be ethically sourced (IBM Diversity in Faces Database), may not be diverse enough (National Institute of Science and Technology FERET Database), or may only consist of famous people's facial images (University of Massachusett LFW Database). There are facial image databases that do not suffer from these issues but are relatively smaller in their participant size (Tufts Face Database).

4.How will Max Planck Institute for Intelligent System use the images?
With the help of the legal and ethical team at the Max Planck Society, we have made sure that your data is collected and saved securely, and protected by GDPR. The dataset will be available upon request to any researcher around the globe. The dataset will not be shared with any third parties for any potential commercial purposes. The dataset will be used in a plethora of research applications from computer vision to human aspects of machine learning.
5.Why is it important to take the images after a 2-months interval?
By collecting photos of the same person every 2-months for 3 times, we hope to increase the variability on the participants' visual cues (hairstyle, clothes, make up, jewelery, etc). Variability in the dataset will enable researchers to build more reliable machine learning systems.
