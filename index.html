What is the problem?

AI systems can be trained to exclude certain individuals if datasets provided for training do not include specific demographic groups, such as underrepresented gender or ethnic groups. These are examples of computational and statistical sources of bias. FRS' level of performance can only be as well as what inputs it is given (e.g., training datasets). If a training database is not representative of diverse demographics, the accuracy of a FRS is highly affected. Unfortunately, databases that include a number of diversity dimensions (e.g., gender, race/ethnicity, age) do not widely exist For example, of about 100 databases currently existing for academic purposes, only two have a representation of Black women and men with over 20 participants of each demographic. Furthermore, of the databases available, several rely on images that were sourced unethically (e.g., taken from the internet websites without individuals’ consent1).

Our Solution to Improve Fairness

Due to a lack of diversity in existing training datasets, our research collaboration team has the goal to build up a diverse and ethically sourced database. !


The objective of this project is to develop a diverse database of facial images that include a larger representation of underrepresented demographics. The database will focus on providing facial images, with the following dimensions of diversity: race/ethnicity, skin color, age, and gender. In contrast to the existing databases that have reportedly misidentified some images in terms of race/ethnicity, gender and age.

Who are we?

Get to know us better

Our team is comprised of two research groups from the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems located in Stuttgart and Tübingen, Germany. The Human Aspects of Machine Learning Research Group investigates the interactions between humans and AI and uses research findings to design AI systems that augment humans’ abilities, rather than replace them. The Organizational Leadership and Diversity Research Group investigates ways in which leaders can be supported by the use of AI to contribute to more tolerant, diverse, and inclusive organizations. The core principles of our research collaboration are to maintain ethical integrity, fairness, transparency, and accountability. Being a diverse group of researchers and bringing together our individual experience, we believe we have the ability to develop effective solutions to the problem of biased AI.
